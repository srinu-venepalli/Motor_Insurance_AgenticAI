# Motor Third-Party Liability Insurance Policy
**Product:** Motor Third-Party | **Version:** v1.0 | **Document ID:** motor_third_party_v1_0

## Section 1: Scope of Cover

### Clause TP-1.0: Third-Party Liability
This policy covers legal liability for death, bodily injury, or property damage caused to third parties arising from the use of the insured vehicle, as mandated under the Motor Vehicles Act. There is no upper limit on liability for third-party death or bodily injury; property damage liability is capped as per the current statutory limit.

### Clause TP-2.0: No Own Damage Cover
This policy does not cover damage to the insured vehicle itself, whether from collision, theft, fire, natural calamity, or any other cause. Own Damage cover (see the Motor Comprehensive policy, Clauses OD-1.0 to OD-4.2) is not included under this product.

### Clause TP-3.0: No Glass, Accessory, or Rental Cover
Clauses relating to glass/windscreen cover, fitted accessories, and rental/replacement vehicle benefits under the Motor Comprehensive policy do not apply to this product.

### Clause PA-1.0: Personal Accident Cover
A statutory personal accident cover applies to the named owner-driver in the event of death or permanent disability arising from an accident involving the insured vehicle, at the sum insured mandated by regulation.

## Section 2: Claims Process

### Clause CL-1.0: Claim Intimation
Any incident giving rise to a potential third-party claim must be reported to the insurer within 48 hours of occurrence.

### Clause CL-2.0: Claim Documentation
A completed claim form, copy of the driving license, registration certificate, and FIR (where applicable) must be submitted within 7 days of intimation. Third-party claims additionally require any correspondence or legal notice received from the third party or their representative.

### Clause CL-5.0: Third-Party Claim Litigation
Where a third-party claim proceeds to a Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT), the insurer will appoint legal representation on behalf of the policyholder and manage the litigation process directly.

## Section 3: Exclusions

### Clause EX-1.0: General Exclusions
This policy does not cover: driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs; driving without a valid license; use of the vehicle for purposes other than those declared in the policy; and war, nuclear risk, or acts contemplated under standard market exclusion clauses.

## Section 4: Policy Validity and Renewal

### Clause PV-1.0: Policy Period
This policy is valid for a period of 12 months from the start date stated in the policy schedule.

### Clause PV-2.0: Grace Period
A grace period of 15 days from the expiry date is available for renewal without fresh underwriting, provided no claim was made during the grace period itself.

### Clause PV-3.0: Cancellation
Either party may cancel this policy by giving 15 days' written notice, subject to the applicable statutory minimum third-party cover requirements.

## Section 5: Upgrading to Comprehensive Cover

### Clause UP-1.0: Mid-Term Upgrade
A policyholder may upgrade from Third-Party to Comprehensive cover at any point during the policy period, subject to a fresh underwriting assessment and a pro-rated additional premium for Own Damage cover from the date of upgrade.
