# Motor Comprehensive Insurance Policy
**Product:** Motor Comprehensive | **Version:** v3.2 | **Document ID:** motor_comprehensive_v3_2

## Section 1: What This Policy Covers

### Clause OD-1.0: Own Damage Cover
This policy covers accidental physical damage to your insured vehicle caused by collision, overturning, fire, self-ignition, explosion, or malicious acts, subject to the excess amount stated in your policy schedule.

### Clause OD-2.0: Theft Cover
Total loss of the insured vehicle due to theft is covered, provided an FIR (First Information Report) is filed with police within 24 hours of discovery and a certified copy is submitted with the claim.

### Clause OD-3.0: Natural Calamity Cover
Damage caused by flood, cyclone, earthquake, landslide, or other natural disasters notified by the relevant state or national disaster authority is covered under Own Damage, subject to policy excess.

### Clause OD-4.0: Parking and Stationary Vehicle Damage
Damage sustained while the vehicle is parked and stationary (not in motion, not being driven) is covered under this section.

#### Clause OD-4.1: Parked Vehicle — Third Party Collision
If a third-party vehicle or object collides with your parked vehicle, own damage cover applies. The customer must obtain, where possible, the registration number or identifying details of the third party for the claim file.

#### Clause OD-4.2: Parked Vehicle — Unknown Cause
Own damage cover applies to collision or impact damage sustained while the vehicle is parked, even where the responsible party cannot be identified, subject to the standard policy excess and a mandatory claim inspection by an authorized surveyor.

### Clause GL-1.0: Glass, Fittings, and Accessories
Windscreen, window glass, and factory-fitted accessories are covered under this policy.

#### Clause GL-2.0: Windscreen and Window Glass Cover
Damage to the windscreen or window glass, including chips and cracks, is covered.

#### Clause GL-2.1: Glass Cover — Standalone Claim
A windscreen or glass-only claim may be filed independently of any other Own Damage claim and does not, by itself, affect the customer's No Claim Bonus (see Clause NCB-3.0), provided no other component of the vehicle was damaged in the same incident.

### Clause RC-1.0: Rental / Replacement Vehicle Cover
Where the insured vehicle is undergoing repair for a covered Own Damage claim exceeding 3 days of estimated workshop time, the policyholder is entitled to a replacement rental vehicle of equivalent or lower class for up to 5 days, subject to availability through the insurer's approved rental partner network. This benefit does not apply to claims solely under Clause GL-2.0 (glass-only claims).

## Section 2: Personal and Third-Party Cover

### Clause TP-1.0: Third-Party Liability
This policy covers legal liability for death, bodily injury, or property damage caused to third parties by the insured vehicle, as mandated under the Motor Vehicles Act. There is no upper limit on liability for third-party death or bodily injury; property damage liability is capped as per the current statutory limit.

### Clause PA-1.0: Personal Accident Cover
A personal accident cover of the sum insured stated in the policy schedule applies to the named owner-driver in the event of death or permanent disability arising from an accident involving the insured vehicle.

## Section 3: No Claim Bonus (NCB)

### Clause NCB-1.0: NCB Eligibility
A No Claim Bonus discount on renewal premium applies for each consecutive year the policyholder does not raise an Own Damage claim, on a sliding scale from 20% (after 1 claim-free year) up to a maximum of 50% (after 5 or more claim-free years).

### Clause NCB-2.0: NCB Forfeiture
Raising more than one Own Damage claim in a policy year resets the NCB slab to 0% at the next renewal.

### Clause NCB-3.0: NCB and Glass-Only Claims
A standalone glass/windscreen claim under Clause GL-2.1 does not count against the NCB slab and does not trigger forfeiture under Clause NCB-2.0.

## Section 4: Claims Process

### Clause CL-1.0: Claim Intimation
Any incident giving rise to a potential claim must be reported to the insurer within 48 hours of occurrence, either through the mobile app, the customer support line, or in writing.

### Clause CL-2.0: Claim Documentation
A completed claim form, copy of the driving license, registration certificate, and (where applicable) an FIR or a garage repair estimate must be submitted within 7 days of intimation.

### Clause CL-3.0: Surveyor Inspection
For claims exceeding the amount specified in the policy schedule, an independent surveyor appointed by the insurer must inspect the vehicle before repair authorization is granted.

### Clause CL-4.0: Claim Settlement Timeline
Once all required documentation is received and the surveyor's report is filed, the insurer will settle or reject the claim within 30 working days.

## Section 5: Exclusions

### Clause EX-1.0: General Exclusions
This policy does not cover: damage arising from driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs; damage where the driver did not hold a valid driving license at the time of the incident; consequential loss or depreciation; mechanical or electrical breakdown not caused by an insured peril; use of the vehicle for purposes other than those declared in the policy (e.g., commercial use of a private vehicle); and war, nuclear risk, or acts contemplated under standard market exclusion clauses.

### Clause EX-2.0: Wear and Tear
Normal wear and tear, gradual deterioration, rust, or corrosion is not covered under this policy.

## Section 6: Policy Validity and Renewal

### Clause PV-1.0: Policy Period
This policy is valid for a period of 12 months from the start date stated in the policy schedule, unless cancelled earlier in accordance with Clause PV-3.0.

### Clause PV-2.0: Grace Period
A grace period of 15 days from the expiry date is available for renewal without a fresh underwriting inspection, provided no claim was made during the grace period itself. Coverage during the grace period is not automatic and applies only once the renewal premium is received and a new policy is issued.

### Clause PV-3.0: Cancellation
Either party may cancel this policy by giving 15 days' written notice. Refund of premium on cancellation is on a short-period scale, provided no claim has been made during the policy period.
